Crucial Difference: The Financial System

  1. Greek banks are illiquid and possibly insolvent
  2. Greek banking system highly vulnerable to Greek sovereign default
  3. South African banks are in a much more robust position
  4. Biggest threat to SA banks: sudden reversal of international capital flows

What Will Happen Next? - South Africa

Recent Developments in SA:

"Today’s budget is constrained by the need to consolidate our public finances, in the context of slower growth and rising debt [...]"

Nhlanhla Nene, Minister of Finance, 2015 budget speech to parliament

  1. Expected budget deficit of 3.9% of GDP for 2015/16 due to weak economic growth. (Reuters)
  2. The government’s debt stock will increase by about 550 billion rand ($48 billion) over the next three years to 2.3 trillion rand by 2017/18

Short-Term Outlook for SA:

  1. Substantial debt repayments necessitated raising additional revenue
  2. Government debt stock will increase by R550bn until 2017/2018
  3. R25bn spending cuts over the next two years, but core social programs will be maintained

"Priorities include overcoming the debilitating problem of electricity shortages, improving industrial relations and the functioning of the labor market, enhancing education and training quality and reducing skill mismatches, boosting competition and trade, and facilitating new firms’ entry."

David Lipton, First Deputy Managing Director at the IMF, 6 March 2015

Long-Term Outlook for SA:

  1. New social policies proposed in the National Development Plan will intensify pressure on government budget


Increased spending needs require

  1. Acceleration of growth
  2. Increase in the tax base
  3. Focusing on core new policies (i.e. education)

Lessons for South Africa

6 Lessons for South Africa

  1. External debt needs to be manageable, even if GDP drops
  2. Persistent current account deficits make the financial system vulnerable
  3. Debt financing for domestic and government consumption is bad
  4. Management of parastatals must be improved
  5. Tax based should be broadened by increasing tax compliance
  6. Austerity measures must be complemented by growth policies
